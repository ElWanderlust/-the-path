Muay Thai, Brazillian Jiu Jitsu to begin with. Become a ghost, water-like. When opponent is strong, disappear. When opponent is weak, comeback. Goal is never to go to war. Goal is guerilla warfare.
It is imperative you study, an hour a week.
Make notes from both study and feedback from training. Reflect and amend accordingly.

Dissolve the ego. Attack, and counterattack. Aggression matters. Have some.
Be able to explode from relaxation. Multiple times. Anaerobic + aerobic.
Mindfulness is key for success. The ability to do without doing.
In training - surrender to the process. You'll have good and bad days. Provide concentrated effort and stay in the game. There is no true winning.

This is not about belts or titles or going pro or whatever other notions of glory you may have. It's about staying a course. It's about following the path.
Scorn street fights and all that other shit. Violence in the streets is not cool . It's stupid. You either run away, only defend yourself in times of no other option. This shit is not a game. But do compete a few times, at the very least. This shit will teach you a lot about yourself. Not in an immature, fight-club manner. But in an honest way.
At the very minimum do some BJJ rolling throughout your life.
